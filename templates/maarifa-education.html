Project Description

Problem Statement

The client (Kenyatta University, Nairobi Kenya) had a challenge of monitoring lecture attendance for students and lecturers as the norm could not meet their intention in quick analysis of the data and providing accurate data. The client needed a software system that could handle both monitoring of the data and fast way to evaluate the data and post it in the students and lecturers portal. The main system specifications were that it should be able to handle monitoring of the attendance even if the students have a feature phone or no phone at all and should be fast i.e. it takes a maximum of 2 minutes to ensure the attendance is captured so as not to disrupt the class for a long time.

Key Steps

Having understood the clients need and the timelines involved towards testing and delivering the system ( 1 semester == 3 months). I broke down the development of the system into 4 main ares.
i) The Unstructured Supplementary Service Data (USSD) application.
Through this application the students with feature phones would be able to mark attendance and be logged in as present during the lecture. The ussd application was developed in python and flask framework.
ii) The mobile application
The mobile app, students with smart-phones were able to mark attendance and be logged in as present during the lecturer. The mobile application was developed in Flutter for both the android and IOS operating systems.
iii) The Web application Portal
The web app portal allowed the administrators of the system to monitor how each class being attended and get the analytics of both the lecturers and students average attendance. It also provided an interface for scheduling and rescheduling of the classes as it was among the features the clients had requested.
iv) The IOT Device
The IOT device allowed for students who had a challenge of not having both a smart-phone or a feature phone to be able to log into the class. Students fingerprints were captured and stored in the database and while in class it was just a matter of confirmation of the prints.

Deliverables

The application development was successful. I was able to test it with school of medicine students and lecturers and got feedback on areas to improve before rolling it out to the rest of the university for use. As this was a big challenge given the timelines involved, The major lesson I learned was that it is important to view each client as a partner rather than another customer and always have a constant communication to check whether you are meeting their needs. Because of the good work I did for the client I was able to get another project from the client. More details about it here.
This project also posed a challenge to my leadership skills. With a team of 3 developers I had to effectively manage their schedule to ensure there is not burnout and at the same time get quality work from them. Breaking down the project really helped as each developer understood their roles perfectly and the best part about this project is that we delivered 3 week before the deadline and that allowed us to get feedback before conducting the test.
